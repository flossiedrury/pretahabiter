TAXIDERMY

Home / Interiors / Taxidermy

Taxidermy 12th August 2018 By Prêt à Habiter

Living with the Dead

Taxidermy is not for everyone, but if it is your thing, it adds a huge amount of originality and magic to a room. I have a morbid obsession with it, from skeletons and skulls to fur coated fluffers. The creativity that surrounds taxidermy is also really excites me. From small insects, such as butterflies, to huge Saharan beasts, the options are almost endless.

Remember: when choosing taxidermy, do not be afraid to mix and match!

It may be less daunting to stick with a particular region and in this way, you can match the taxidermy to the room. For example, if decking out a Bothy, you may want to stick with species that would be found in Scotland, such as badgers, foxes and endemic fish species. But I personally love to mix and match from all areas - if I see a piece that looks like it has personality, I add it to my collection! I was recently inspired to expand my collection when visiting a Thai restaurant in Hammersmith (Poppy's 2 - check it out!), where every wall is covered with taxidermy, from zebra to unicorns.

Another tip - go faux! This will definitely help your pocket (and maybe your conscience too). Taxidermy can be extremely expensive, particularly for the larger pieces, so if your dream is to cover a whole wall like mine is, faux is probably the way forward.

Top Tips:

1. A plain background, such as a white or pale painted wall, will do your taxidermy picks most justice

2. If going statment, choose a large piece and place it centrally - above a fireplace always looks special

3. If going mix and match, go crazy! Different sizes, colours, shapes and textures will make your room look fun and intruging (as opposed to murderous and creepy)

4. Not all taxidermy has to be wall mounted. If you're unsure to begin with, items in jars or framed butterflies may be the way to start

I reccomend searching at car boot sales and antiques markets - occasionally we have haggled and got a piece of taxidermy chucked in for free when buying other furniture. Alternatively, you can make your own (hopeully faux) pieces - have a look at https://www.thegatheredhome.com/diy-gilded-insect-plaques/

For some inspiration, I would reccommend the taxidermy section on Pinterest and for a laugh, check out http://crappytaxidermy.com/
